Jade Anastasia Akinyo
Fifth Grade Teacher

Hello, my name is Jade Anastasia Akinyo and I'm currently a 5th grade self-contained teacher in Abuja, Nigeria. I've been teaching internationally for the past 5 years and have been an elementary teacher for over 10 years. I strive to challenge, engage and push my students to full potential both socially and academically. This website is a showcase of my work as an educator at my current school in Abuja, Nigeria.

About My Classroom

In my classroom, I strive for high academic achievement through project-based learning, advanced critical thinking skills, communication of ideas and rigorous problem-solving activities. Students in my classroom are exposed to a wide variety of real-world discussion and advocacy opportunities in order to increase self-esteem through self-expression. In my classroom I provide opportunities for students to connect the influences of Nigerian culture through all academic areas to promote social responsibility. Students explore the role of cultural diversity and how it plays in the human experience. Currently I teach 5th grade self-contained and have 22 students in my classroom.

My Teaching Philosophy

In my classroom, I strive to offer my students a high-quality American education, infusing international content wherever possible in order to reflect our diverse student population and to give the program international relevance.

In my classroom, students are challenged to maximize their potential for learning through instruction, assessment, accountability protocols and initiatives. Such initiatives have included development of assignment and course rubrics, development of narratives for communicating student achievement and based on achievement against course standards, teacher accountability to curricular and instructional expectations, and the implementation of research-based strategies.

I also strongly believe that influencing students with technology integration is to enhance and expand the learning process. My philosophy is to integrate technology that supports learning through meaningful integration allowing the student to retain the knowledge and skills taught rather than teaching tech skills in isolation which can lead to forgetfulness. The purpose of integration in my classroom is to use technological tools to advance student understanding of the academic objectives.

Culturally Responsive Teaching

It's so important to incorporate Culturally Responsive teaching in your classroom and I'm proud and ecstatic that it's a huge part of my classroom culture. Culturally Responsive Teaching truly creates meaningful and useful connections in the classroom and embodies the beauty of having a diverse and open-minded environment. My classroom represents many different languages, cultures and religions where students are able to make connections and find relevancy in lessons and higher-level academic skills.

Using Culturally Responsive Teaching (CRT) in my classroom helps my students feel empowered by sharing cultures, experiences and shared interests within the community. In this section, I will explain how developing the trust and curriculum within my classroom helped strengthen and build student self-confidence centered around student learning.
